My education and skills

Honours Bachelor of Science - Mathematics and Statistics (Fifth Year)

Over the past five years, I have completed many courses at McMaster on data science, mathematical coding, and statistical model construction and validation, wherein we have used various machine and statistical learning methods to model and make predictions using real-world data sets. I am most interested in imaging data and working with segmentation and computer vision models.


I have a keen interest in AI. In particular, I am interested in research involving modern AI frameworks to expand my repertoire, as I envision myself going into related work, likely in healthcare, in the future. I have a well-built theoretical understanding and practical experience with many machine learning methods, including neural networks and vision transformers, as well as several search algorithms including genetic algorithms. I have extensive experience with R and Python. Notably, I am very comfortable with Pytorch/Torch, Caret, Tensorflow, Keras and CUDA.


Most recently, I have been working alongside Dr. Rajeev Muni’s ophthalmology group at St. Michael’s Hospital as both a machine learning student and a data science specialist. Working alongside physicians and other AI students, I have helped create a new computer vision tool to automatically identify, segment, and quantify the total fluid in diseased eyes. This project is intended for publication, as well as implementation into the clinic’s workflow as a clinical decision support tool. In addition, I regularly conduct statistical analyses (ex: ROC tables, significance tests) and advise groups of physicians on appropriate statistical conclusions, all using real patient data.
